Black Soldier Fly Farming Insights

01 Transforming organic waste into valuable animal feed

At EctoFarm, we specialize in turning everyday organic waste into a powerful agricultural resource. Using the natural efficiency of the Black Soldier Fly (BSF), we process food scraps, crop residues, and market waste that would otherwise contribute to pollution. Our BSF larvae consume these materials rapidly, breaking them down into nutrient-rich biomass that can be harvested and transformed into sustainable feed. This process not only minimizes landfill waste but also helps farmers and agribusinesses manage their waste more responsibly. By recycling organic matter into larvae, we close the loop in food production and create a truly circular farming system that benefits both people and the planet.

02 A cost-effective protein source for poultry, pigs, and fish

Black Soldier Fly larvae are nature’s gift to livestock farmers. Containing up to 45% protein, essential amino acids, and healthy fats, they offer a balanced, digestible, and energy-rich diet for animals such as poultry, pigs, and fish. Unlike expensive imported feeds like fishmeal or soy, BSF-based feeds are produced locally and sustainably, drastically lowering feed costs for farmers. EctoFarm’s larvae help improve animal growth rates, feather quality, and overall health, while also enhancing feed conversion ratios. With rising feed costs across the region, our BSF protein solution offers a practical and scalable alternative that supports both smallholder and commercial farmers.

03 Eco-friendly farming that supports circular agriculture

Sustainability is at the heart of what we do. Every kilogram of BSF larvae produced by EctoFarm helps reduce methane emissions from decomposing organic waste. In addition to the larvae, we also produce BSF frass — a natural byproduct that serves as an organic fertilizer rich in nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium. Farmers can use this frass to boost soil fertility, improve crop yields, and minimize dependence on chemical fertilizers.
